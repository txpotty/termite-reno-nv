Detailed Termite Control Services

Our comprehensive termite control services are designed to address every aspect of a termite infestation, from initial detection to long-term prevention. We employ a multi-faceted approach to ensure complete eradication and protection for your property.

Termite Inspection and Identification

The first crucial step in effective termite control is a thorough and accurateinspection. Our trained technicians will meticulously examine your property, both inside and out, to identify signs of termite activity. This includes looking for:

  • Mud Tubes

    These pencil-sized tubes are often found on foundation walls, joists, and other structural elements. Subterranean termites use these tubes to travel between their underground colonies and their food source (your home's wood). Identifying the presence and location of mud tubes is a strong indicator of a termite infestation and helps us understand where the termites are active.

  • Winged Swarmers

    Termite swarmers, also known as alates, are reproductive termites that leave the colony to establish new ones. They are often mistaken for flying ants. Finding discarded wings near windowsills, doors, or light fixtures is a key sign that a termite colony is nearby and has recently swarmed. This indicates a mature colony that is actively reproducing.

  • Damaged Wood

    Wood damaged by termites can appear discolored, hollow-sounding when tapped, or even have visible tunnels or galleries within it. Termites eat the wood from the inside out, often leaving a thin veneer of wood or paint intact. Identifying damaged wood helps us pinpoint the areas where termites are actively feeding and causing damage.

  • Frass (Termite Droppings)

    Drywood termites, unlike subterranean termites, do not require contact with the soil. They expel small, wood-colored fecal pellets called frass. These pellets may accumulate in small piles near infested wood. The presence of frass is a clear sign of drywood termite activity and helps us locate their nesting areas.

Our detailed inspection process allows us to accurately diagnose the type of termites present (subterranean, drywood, or dampwood) and determine the extent of the infestation. This information is critical for developing a customized and effective treatment plan.

Targeted Treatment Plans

Based on the findings of our inspection, we develop a targeted treatment plan designed to eliminate existing termites and protect your home from future infestations. We utilize a combination of proven methods, including:

  • Liquid Termiticides

    Liquid termiticides are applied to the soil around the foundation of your home, creating a protective barrier that termites cannot penetrate. These treatments are highly effective in eliminating subterranean termites by preventing them from reaching your home and by treating existing colonies in the soil. We carefully apply termiticides according to industry best practices and local regulations to ensure safety and maximum effectiveness. The application process is meticulous, ensuring every point of potential entry is treated, establishing a continuous defense around your valuable property.

  • Termite Baiting Systems

    Termite baiting systems utilize bait stations strategically placed around your property. The bait contains a slow-acting insecticide that termites consume and carry back to their colony, effectively eliminating the entire colony over time. This method is environmentally friendly and provides ongoing monitoring and control. Baiting systems are particularly effective for monitoring termite activity and eliminating colonies without extensive disruption to your landscape. The bait is highly palatable to termites, ensuring they readily consume it, leading to a domino effect within the colony as the active ingredient is shared, ultimately leading to the colony's demise. This approach provides a sustainable and long-term solution for termite management.

  • Wood Treatments

    For drywood termites, localized wood treatments may be necessary. These treatments involve applying insecticides directly to infested wood or injecting them into termite galleries. This targets the termites within the wood and prevents further damage. The type of wood treatment used depends on the location and extent of the drywood termite infestation. These treatments are precise and aim to eliminate the termites harbored within the very structures they are damaging, ensuring complete eradication of localized infestations and preserving the structural integrity of your home. We carefully select and apply treatments that are effective against drywood termites while minimizing any impact on your home's aesthetics.

Our goal is to not only eliminate the current termite problem but also to implement measures that provide long-term protection for your home. We will discuss the recommended treatment plan with you, explaining the process and answering any questions you may have. Our transparency ensures you are well-informed and comfortable with the steps we are taking to protect your home.

Common Termite Problems and How We Help

Understanding the common signs of a termite infestation is crucial for early detection and intervention. Unfortunately, termites are often undetected until significant damage has occurred. Here's a look at some common termite problems homeowners face and how Reno Termite provides effective solutions:

  • Discovering Mud Tubes on Your Foundation

    Mud tubes are one of the most visible signs of subterranean termites. These tubes serve as protected pathways allowing termites to travel from their moist soil environment to the wood of your home. Finding these tubes on your foundation, walls, or even within crawl spaces is a strong indicator of active termite foraging. Ignoring mud tubes allows termites to continue their destructive work unchecked. Reno Termite conducts thorough inspections to locate all mud tubes, both visible and hidden, and implements targeted treatments to eliminate the termite colony responsible for building them. Our treatments address the termites at their source, disrupting their access points and preventing further damage. We also provide recommendations for reducing moisture around your foundation, which helps deter future mud tube construction.

  • Finding Discarded Termite Wings

    After a termite swarm, which is a reproductive flight to establish new colonies, you may find piles of discarded wings near windows, doorways, or light fixtures. These wings are a clear indication that a mature termite colony is present nearby and the swarmers have completed their dispersal flight. While finding wings doesn't mean your home is currently infested, it definitely means that a termite colony is in the vicinity and your home could be a target. Reno Termite’s experts can determine if these wings are from termites and conduct a thorough inspection to evaluate the risk to your property. If an active infestation is found, we will implement a comprehensive treatment plan to eliminate the colony and protect your home from future swarming events. Early detection based on discarded wings can prevent extensive damage down the line.

  • Hollow-Sounding Wood

    Termites eat wood from the inside out, leaving only a thin veneer. This hidden damage can make wooden structures sound hollow when tapped. Areas like door frames, window sills, and baseboards are particularly susceptible. If you tap on wood and it sounds hollow or feels soft, it could be a sign of termite damage. Our technicians are skilled at identifying the subtle signs of termite damage, including hollow-sounding wood. We use specialized tools and techniques to pinpoint infested areas and determine the extent of the damage. Our treatment plans are then customized to address these specific areas, eliminating the termites and preventing further deterioration of your home's structure. Repairing or replacing termite-damaged wood may also be necessary after treatment, and we can provide guidance on this process.

  • Sagging Floors or Ceilings

    In severe cases of termite infestation and damage, structural elements like floors or ceilings may begin to sag. This is a serious sign of extensive wood destruction and requires immediate attention. Termite damage compromises the integrity of the wood, leading to weakening and eventual structural failure if left untreated. If you notice any unexplained sagging in your floors or ceilings, it is crucial to contact a professional termite control service immediately. Reno Termite understands the urgency of addressing structural damage caused by termites. We will quickly assess the situation, implement a rapid and effective treatment plan to eliminate the termites, and provide recommendations for structural repairs to restore the safety and stability of your home. Addressing sagging structures due to termites is not just about pest control, but also about ensuring the safety of everyone in your home.

  • Bubbling or Peeling Paint

    Sometimes, bubbling or peeling paint can indicate the presence of termites behind the wall. As termites tunnel through the wood, they can create conditions that affect the paint or wallpaper on the surface. This can happen particularly where termites are working close to the surface of the wood. While bubbling or peeling paint can have other causes, if you observe this in conjunction with other potential termite signs, it warrants investigation. Our experienced technicians can distinguish between paint issues and potential termite indicators. We will carefully examine the affected areas to determine if termites are the underlying cause and implement the appropriate treatment. Addressing the termite issue is necessary before attempting any repairs to the paint or wall surface, as the problem will reoccur if the pests are still active.
